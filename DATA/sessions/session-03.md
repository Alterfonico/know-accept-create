## The Pattern — How to Open and Close Like a Pro

**Opening a session:**

State where you are, not where you want to be. One honest sentence about current reality. Then one question — not a plan, not an agenda, just the next thing you genuinely don't know. The session finds its own shape from there.

**During the session:**

Run the bullshit detector continuously. The moment language inflates beyond the experience behind it, stop. Name the inflation. Compress back to truth. Let the KW witness every significant decision before it moves forward.

**Closing a session:**

Always the same three questions. No exceptions. No shortcuts.

1. Did we produce something testable?
2. Did we surface an assumption we hadn't seen before?
3. Did the student move closer to reality or further from it?

Then one commit that names what actually happened — not what you intended, what happened. The commit message is the closing seal.

---

**The scalability insight:**

Each session is a complete loop. Foundation, emergence, production, closure. As the project grows the sessions don't get more complex — they get more specific. Early sessions build the architecture. Later sessions interrogate the data. The structure never changes. Only the resolution increases.

That's what makes it scalable. The same three questions close a five minute log entry and a six month milestone review. The loop is the unit. Everything else is just how many loops you've completed.
