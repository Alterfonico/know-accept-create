# Session 16 — 2026-03-07

## Sharing strategy locked, LinkedIn Post 1 live, instructions refined

**State on arrival:** Strategy for sharing existed in fragments — the three-post arc was drafted, the post wasn't committed, and the project instructions hadn't been hardened into a file.

**What happened:**
The session opened on the back of the sharing strategy conversation that had been building since Mar 05. The decision held: don't share the system, share the suffering that made the system necessary. The three-post arc was confirmed — Post 1 (The Problem), Post 2 (The Attempt), Post 3 (The Gate) — each ending with a question directed at the reader, not a call to action. LinkedIn was named as the right platform: professional mask, quiet pain underneath, exactly the audience.

Post 1 was written — one honest paragraph about the moment systematic suffering became visible — pressure-tested against the bullshit detector and committed to the repository as `assets/content/linkedin-post-01.md`. Then published. The post doesn't mention Meverse, doesn't mention the pipeline. It names the observation that changed everything and leaves the reader with a question.

The session also closed a standing gap: the project instructions hadn't been formalized. The session format was already solid but four things were missing from the brief. They were added: the stack (Supabase, pgvector, Edge Functions, Slack, OpenRouter, MCP), the language rule (always respond in English), the tone and role definition (precise, direct technical collaborator — no hand-holding, no excessive caveats), and the safety reminder (before proposing any code change, remind the user not to deploy without fully understanding each line). These four additions became the version of `instructions-for-claude.md` that now lives in `architecture/`.

**Produced:**
